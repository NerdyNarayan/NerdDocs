
###  **Getting Started with Go**

If you're totally new to Go, start here:

- 🏁 **[Go Tour](https://go.dev/tour/welcome/1)**: An interactive tour of Go, providing a solid introduction to the language and its core features.
- 📚 **[Golang Docs](https://golangdocs.com)**: My choice for understanding the fundamentals of go.
- 💡 **[Go by Example](https://gobyexample.com)**: A  introduction to Go, with examples covering the essential aspects of the language.
- 📚 **[Standard Library](https://golang.org/pkg/#stdlib)**: Familiarize yourself with Go’s standard library to avoid unnecessary dependencies.
- 💬 **[Code Review Comments](https://go.dev/wiki/CodeReviewComments)**: A complementary document to "Effective Go," full of valuable tips for code reviews and writing better Go code.
- 📜 **[Go Language Specification](https://golang.org/ref/spec)**: The official language specification is your go-to for understanding the core aspects of Go.

### 🚀 **Build Projects**

After getting the basics down, start building projects:

- 🎥 **[Learn Go Programming by Building 11 Projects](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jFfo23yIWac)**: A full course on YouTube, guiding you through the process of building 11 projects in Go.
- 🔨 **[Project-Based Learning in Go](https://github.com/practical-tutorials/project-based-learning?tab=readme-ov-file#go)**: A collection of practical tutorials and projects to enhance your Go programming skills.
- 🛠️ **[Golang Mini Projects](https://github.com/akilans/golang-mini-projects)**: A collection of mini-projects to help you practice and improve your Go programming skills.
- 🤖 **[Automate Go Projects](https://github.com/dreddsa5dies/automateGo/tree/master/projects)**: A repository containing projects to help you automate tasks and workflows using Go.
- 🛠️ **[Gophercises](https://gophercises.com/)**: A collection of guided, long-ish tutorials to enhance your Go skills.

### 📖 **Books **

- 📚 **[Golang Books](https://github.com/NerdyNarayan/NerdResources/tree/main/Golang/Books)**: A curated list of books for learning and mastering Go, hosted on GitHub.
- 📚 **[The Interpreter/Compiler Book](https://interpreterbook.com/)**: A guide to writing an interpreter, with a sequel focused on compilers. Definitely on my "to-do" list.
- 📚 **[Practical Go Lessons](https://www.practical-go-lessons.com)**: A massive book covering everything you need to know about writing Go. The HTML version is free, but you can support the author by purchasing physical or digital copies. A must-bookmark!


### 💡 **Tips, Tricks, and Common Gotchas**

Writing idiomatic Go code requires knowing its quirks:

- 🌑 **[The Darker Corners of Go](https://rytisbiel.com/2021/03/06/darker-corners-of-go/)**: An article exploring some of the lesser-known but important aspects of Go.
- 🕶️ **[50 Shades of Go](http://devs.cloudimmunity.com/gotchas-and-common-mistakes-in-go-golang/)**: An article highlighting common mistakes and gotchas in Go.
